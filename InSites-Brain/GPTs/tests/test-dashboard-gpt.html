A 19th-century mechanized flour mill founded by German Templer settlers in the Haifa colony. The mill operated continuously through Ottoman, British Mandate, and Israeli periods, surviving a major fire during the 1936 Arab Revolt. It stands as one of the few remaining examples of early industrial infrastructure in the Levant.

Cultural Significance Statement

The Old Mill represents a rare surviving example of 19th-century mechanized flour production in the Levant, embodying the technological transfer brought by Templer settlers.
